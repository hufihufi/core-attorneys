Switzerland is set to introduce a foreign direct investment (FDI) screening regime, joining a growing number of countries that review cross-border acquisitions for national security risks.

## Background

The Federal Council has proposed legislation that would require pre-notification and approval for acquisitions of Swiss companies in sensitive sectors by foreign investors. The proposed regime follows similar frameworks already in place in the EU, UK, Germany and the United States.

## Key features of the proposed regime

The draft legislation targets acquisitions in sectors considered critical to national security and public order, including:

- Energy infrastructure
- Water and food supply
- Healthcare and pharmaceuticals
- Telecommunications and digital infrastructure
- Defence and security

## Implications for transactions

Companies planning acquisitions of Swiss businesses in covered sectors should factor potential FDI screening into their transaction timelines. Pre-merger coordination with COMCO requirements may also arise in parallel.
